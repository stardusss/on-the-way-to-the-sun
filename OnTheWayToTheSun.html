Stardust Team Presents:

The Parker Space Probe’s Journey To The Sun

An Overview of The Probe and its Journey:

1. Parker space probe was launched on August 12 2018.

2. The mission of the parker space probe is to explore the heating or the corona given that it is hotter than the sun’s surface, certain behaviors of the magnetic field at the origins of the solar wind, and acceleration of certain particles. It also aims to provide way to protect modern technology from any future Carrington effects.

3. The probe is designated to go in oval shaped orbits between Venus and the sun that narrows down with every passing by the probe makes to Venus.

4. It “touched the sun” on April 28 2021.

5. With its travels, the parker solar probe has collected a lot of data related to the sun, Venus and other areas

Challenges faced:

1. The first challenge faced by parker solar probe is how it may travel to the direction of the sun: a. Since the earth rotates around the sun, the space craft would launch in the same direction as earth’s solar orbit. This would propel the probe away from the sun so to prevent this a counter force should be provided in order to redirect it towards the sun’s orbit.

b. Venus would over the course of many years provide the necessary gravitational force to help the space probe move in a oval shaped orbit towards the sun and back to Venus.

c. Despite the slowing down of the sideways motion, the overall speed of the probe would increase with every close encounter with the sun.

2. Another challenge for the probe was providing the means to protect it from the radiation and the extreme temperatures of the sun.

a. A carbon-composite “Thermal Protection System” (TPS) was provided as a heat shield for the probe for equipment protection against extreme heats and energy as well as hypervelocity particles. The parts that were not protected would use metals with high boiling points such as niobium for the wiring, an alloy for molybdenum for the Faraday cups, etc.


b. The density of the corona itself which, despite the corona being hotter than the surface of the sun, will not heat up the solar probe as much as the surface due to the spread out nature of the plasma in that layer.

c. An extensive cooling system involving 3.7 liters of deionized water (around a gallon), this water would circulate to radiators carrying along with it heat which would be radiated into deep space while preserving the water itself. Water is the best coolant for the space probe since when pressurized enough it could withstand temperatures as extreme as 125 C.

3. Yet another challenge was navigation, since the probe would travel very far away from the earth and since it would approach the sun which has a very strong magnetic field, manual control of the probe from earth was an extremely difficult prospect. The solution for this setback was to set up multiple small sensors on the body of the spacecraft and are attached to the central computer. If sunlight is detected by any of the sensors, the probe will adjust its position relative to the sun.

Data Collected:

1. Solar Wind Boundary: In what was called “The Solar Wind Electrons Alphas and Protons investigation” (SWEAP), a boundary where solar material escapes the sun and becomes solar wind would be established. The surprising discovery was that the boundary was not smooth at all but wrinkly and rough. In addition, a closer look at the sun’s streamers was achieved. All these would not have been easily observed had it not been for the probe’s entry into the corona of the sun.

2. The Dust Free Zone: The Wide Imager for Parker Solar Probe (WISPR) would hint at a prediction proposed by scientist Henry Norris Russell stating that there would be a zone near the sun void of any dust since that dust in that region should vaporize due to the extreme temperatures. The WISPR would observe the dimming of light reflection caused by mentioned dust at 19 solar radii hinting at a depletion of dust when the probe approaches closer to the sun. A total confirmation of the prediction would be achieved if in about 5 solar radii, there would be no presence of dust thus confirming the dust free zone.

3. Magnetic Reversals: Though this field is still under research, a bit of information has surfaced that deepened our knowledge about the sun’s magnetic field and a possible power source for the solar wind. The measurements indicated that the magnetic field exhibits rapid flips shaped similarly to zig-zag mountain roads. During the 6th visit to the sun, FIELDS data showed magnetic funnels emerging between super granules on the sun.

4. Additional research: In addition to these three, parker solar probe provided material related to Venus revealing more details about its surface temperature as well as the cooler regions and the hotter regions, some even suspect the planet may have been habitable before opening up the door for more planetary observations to come.


The Parker Solar probe continues to provide a clearer overview about space weather, the sun’s magnetic field and other research material related to other celestial bodies. The journey of the spacecraft has not ended yet and more doors for further scientific endeavors will open in the future rotations of the probe
